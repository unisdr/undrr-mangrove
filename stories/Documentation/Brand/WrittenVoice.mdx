# Written voice

UNDRR has one voice across every property. What changes from page to page is *register*, not voice. This page covers the four registers — urgent, authoritative, warm, hopeful — and when each is right.

For sentence-level mechanics (active voice, plain language, error messages, link text, gender-neutral terminology, accessibility), the source of truth is the <LinkTo kind="contributing-writing-guidelines" story="docs">writing guidelines</LinkTo>. Read that first; this page sits on top of it.

## The voice (constant)

UNDRR's voice is anchored to the brand positioning trio — **Knowledgeable, Approachable, Collaborative** — defined canonically in <LinkTo kind="brand-brand-guidelines" story="docs">brand guidelines</LinkTo>. Every UNDRR sentence should ladder back to at least one of the three. If it doesn't, rewrite it.

In verbal terms, that means: Knowledgeable shows up as sourced numbers and primary citations (never rounded paraphrases); Approachable as plain language, no jargon, active voice; Collaborative as speaking *with* the reader, naming partners, avoiding the institutional passive.

---

## The four registers

Register is how voice meets the moment. The voice is the same; the temperature changes.

### 1. Urgent

When: live disasters, early warnings, time-sensitive calls to action, evacuation guidance, "what to do in the next hour" content.

How it sounds:

- Front-loaded. The verb arrives in the first six words.
- Imperative. "Move to higher ground." "Tune in to local radio."
- Concrete. Specific actions, specific times, specific places.
- Restrained. Urgency comes from precision, not adjectives. Never "catastrophic devastation"; always "Cyclone X made landfall at 14:30 local time."

Anti-pattern: false urgency on non-urgent content. A campaign launch is not an emergency.

### 2. Authoritative

When: policy summaries, research findings, the Sendai Framework references, official statements, methodology pages, Words into Action guidance.

How it sounds:

- Sourced. Cite the report, the resolution, the dataset.
- Neutral. State what the evidence shows; let the reader draw the conclusion.
- Precise. "The 2015–2030 Sendai Framework" not "the Sendai goals." "The 2024 Global Assessment Report" not "recent UNDRR research."
- Long sentences are allowed if the structure earns them — but break for readability.

Anti-pattern: institutional fog ("it is important to note that"). Cut.

### 3. Warm

When: people stories, community profiles, partner spotlights, recovery narratives, success cases.

How it sounds:

- Named. People have names, places, jobs, ages. "Maria, 34, a community health worker in Talcahuano" beats "a local resident."
- Specific. The mango tree, the school's blue gate, the morning the river rose.
- Quoted. People speak in their own words; quotes are not rewritten for grammatical perfection.
- Emotion at the edges. Let the reader feel without telling them how to feel.

Anti-pattern: humanitarian-poverty-porn. Show agency, not victimhood.

### 4. Hopeful

When: prevention success, recovery progress, framework anniversaries, future-facing communication, IDDRR campaign content.

How it sounds:

- Forward-looking but evidence-based. "Communities reduced flood damage by 38% after installing early warning systems" beats "things are getting better."
- Plural. The progress is collective. UNDRR, partners, member states, communities all named.
- Specific futures. "By 2030" beats "in the coming years."
- Earns optimism. Never glosses over what is still hard.

Anti-pattern: empty optimism. UNDRR is institutionally serious — hope is grounded, not promotional.

## Layered application

The voice trio (Knowledgeable / Approachable / Collaborative) is the *thread* — locked at every layer. Register is the *variable* — what flexes per layer, audience, and moment. For the full threads-and-variables model see <LinkTo kind="brand-layered-identity" story="docs">layered identity</LinkTo>; for per-audience register emphasis (and the per-sub-brand register skew) see <LinkTo kind="brand-audience-mapping" story="docs">audience mapping</LinkTo>.

> **Tip:** When a campaign tone feels off, check whether it has dropped Authoritative entirely. UNDRR campaigns can be warm or hopeful, but they cannot be marketing-flavoured. Credibility is non-negotiable.

## House conventions

These are not stylistic preferences. They are conventions enforced in-tree and in publications.

| Convention | Source |
| --- | --- |
| **Oxford-flavoured British English (UN editorial style)** in documentation, MDX, story titles, error messages, and external publications. Code identifiers (function names, variable names, file names, package names) follow US English to match the JavaScript ecosystem (`color`, not `colour`, in CSS properties and JS APIs). When in doubt, the UN Editorial Manual is the tie-breaker for prose. | UNDRR Policies and SOPs; UN Editorial Manual |
| **Sentence case** for headings, titles, navigation, buttons | <LinkTo kind="contributing-writing-guidelines" story="docs">Writing guidelines</LinkTo>; Material Design |
| **Em-dashes are fine in body prose; never in UI strings, button labels, or story names** | Mangrove house convention; use parentheses for qualifiers in UI |
| **Descriptive link text** — never "click here." In tables, link the row's title rather than adding a trailing "View" column | UN/Microsoft inclusive-communication guidance |
| **Gender-neutral terms** by default ("chair," "spokesperson," "humankind") | <LinkTo kind="contributing-writing-guidelines" story="docs">Writing guidelines</LinkTo>; UN gender-inclusive language guidelines |
| **Disability-inclusive language** | UN Disability Inclusion Strategy; UN Geneva guidelines |
| **Photo credits** always | SharePoint Photography & Icons page |
| **UN docs links** structured `https://www.undocs.org/xx/xx/xxxx` | Policies and SOPs |
| **Country names** from UNTERM | Policies and SOPs |
| **Front-loaded** key info; inverted-pyramid structure | <LinkTo kind="brand-brand-guidelines" story="docs">Brand guidelines</LinkTo>; GOV.UK |

## Common rewrites

| Drift | Rewrite | Why |
| --- | --- | --- |
| "Stakeholders are encouraged to leverage best practices to mainstream DRR." | "Local governments can use the MCR2030 toolkit to plan for floods and heatwaves." | Jargon → plain; abstract → concrete |
| "It has been determined that early warning systems are effective." | "Early warning systems reduced cyclone deaths by 50% across the Pacific between 2014 and 2024 (UNDRR GAR 2024)." | Institutional passive → sourced active |
| "Click here for more information." | "Read the full 2024 Global Assessment Report." | Non-descriptive link → action + source |
| "Mankind faces unprecedented climate-driven risks." | "People everywhere face new climate-driven risks." | Gendered → neutral; emotive adjective → factual |
| "Catastrophic devastation tore through the coastal community, leaving lives shattered." | "Cyclone Y made landfall in Country X on 14 March 2026. Two months later, three of the four worst-hit communities are reporting full water and power restoration. UNDRR is supporting recovery in the fourth alongside [partner]." | Adjective-led drama → specific facts, named partners, evidence of recovery |

## A note on AI-assisted drafting

If you draft headlines, summaries, alt text, or copy with a language model, treat the output as a draft.

- **Run every output through the unified <LinkTo kind="brand-anti-patterns" story="docs">pre-publish checklist</LinkTo>** before publishing.
- **House conventions catch register drift the model introduces.** Sentence case, Oxford-flavoured British English (in prose — not code), descriptive links, em-dash discipline — these are the easiest sieves.
- **Follow institutional AI usage guidance.** The standing rules, institutional sources, and contact paths are documented on the <LinkTo kind="brand-ai-imagery-policy" story="docs">AI imagery policy</LinkTo> page. Copy-and-editorial prompt templates (alt text, headlines, tone register, British-English converter, sentence-case rewriter) live on the <LinkTo kind="brand-ai-imagery-prompts" story="docs">AI imagery prompts</LinkTo> page. When in doubt, ask UNDRR Communications (`undrrcomms@un.org`) before publishing AI-assisted text.
