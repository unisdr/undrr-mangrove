# AI imagery prompts

A working library for turning a rough idea ("I want something about financing for disaster risk reduction") into a UNDRR-brand-compliant prompt. Three ways to use this page:

1. **Look at a holistic example** to see what a finished UNDRR prompt looks like.
2. **Use the formula** — combine your topic with the UNDRR style block.
3. **Use the LLM meta-prompt** — paste your rough brief into ChatGPT or Claude and it writes the image prompt for you.

**First time here?** The LLM meta-prompt (section 3) is the lowest-friction path — it only asks for a rough brief and handles the rest.

<DocCallout variant="warning">
  Read the <LinkTo kind="brand-ai-imagery-policy" story="docs">AI imagery policy</LinkTo> before using anything on this page. The 12 standing rules (no photoreal humans, no children, no rendered text, named country, disability-as-peer, decolonial framing, etc.) are the floor — every prompt here assumes you have read them.
</DocCallout>

## 1. Holistic examples (what "good" looks like)

These three prompts cover the most common UNDRR use cases. Read them to internalise the pattern; then jump to the formula or meta-prompt below to build your own.

### A. Hero illustration with people

> Flat vector editorial illustration of a coastal community planting mangrove seedlings in late afternoon light. Setting: Vanuatu, Efate Island, concrete-block community hall with corrugated-iron roof in mid-ground. Clearly stylised, not photo-realistic; flat fills and soft outlines, no rendered facial detail. Faces in profile, three-quarter view, or obscured by working posture; never frontal portraits. Mixed team: deliberately varied skin tones, ages including older persons, body types; at least one person with a visible disability participating as a peer. Working clothing appropriate to the place; no aid-worker hi-vis vests. Palette: Sendai reduced-saturation (#d45363, #f19e6a, #b669ab, #4dc7c6) plus UNDRR Corporate Blue (#004f91). 16:6 hero banner, focal subject left of centre, hopeful mood, no text, no rendered letters, no logos, no icons.

**Reads as:** Approachable + Collaborative. Negative space right of centre for editorial copy.

### B. Conceptual diagram (no people)

> Flat vector editorial illustration of an early warning system for coastal flooding in Bangladesh, Sundarbans delta. No human figures. Side-elevation perspective, not isometric. Elements: sensor buoy at sea, transmission relay tower, community siren, household receiver showing a flashing visual alert symbol and audible siren waves, TV screen with a closed-captioning pictogram (no rendered words). Clear connecting flow lines. White background. Palette: Sendai reduced-saturation (#d45363, #f19e6a, #b669ab, #4dc7c6) plus UNDRR Corporate Blue (#004f91). 16:9 aspect ratio, no text, no rendered letters, no logos, no icons.

**Reads as:** Knowledgeable. Explains a system without sci-fi tropes; accessibility cues built in.

### C. Infographic background

> Vector infographic background, single horizontal band, palette: Sendai colours at reduced saturation (#d45363, #f19e6a, #b669ab, #4dc7c6), no foreground elements, very subtle texture or gradient suggesting progress over time, suitable as a background behind a sequential data visualisation about Sendai Framework targets, low-contrast so foreground type and chart elements remain readable, 16:6 aspect ratio. No text, no rendered letters, no logos, no icons.

**Reads as:** Institutional, unobtrusive. Signals UNDRR brand without competing with the chart.

### D. People in a professional, non-community setting (the financing / policy case)

> Flat vector editorial illustration of a national-government planning meeting reviewing a multi-year DRR investment plan in Indonesia. Setting: working government office in Jakarta — wooden table, daylight from a side window, printed budget projections and a national risk map laid out. Mixed planners as peers: representatives of the finance ministry, disaster management agency, and planning ministry. Clearly stylised, not photo-realistic; flat fills and soft outlines, no rendered facial detail. Faces in profile or three-quarter view; no frontal portraits. Mixed gender, varied ages including older persons, deliberately varied skin tones reflecting Indonesia's actual diversity (Javanese, Sundanese, Batak, Bugis, Papuan and other heritage groups). At least one person with a visible disability participating as a peer (for example a wheelchair user at the table or a participant using a hearing aid). Working clothing appropriate to a government office — no aid-worker hi-vis vests. Palette: Sendai reduced-saturation (#d45363, #f19e6a, #b669ab, #4dc7c6) plus UNDRR Corporate Blue (#004f91). 16:6 hero banner, focal subject left of centre, negative space right of centre for editorial copy, no text, no rendered letters, no logos, no icons.

**Reads as:** Knowledgeable + Collaborative. Avoids the conference-room and "outside expert / local recipient" traps that this kind of subject usually falls into.

## 2. The formula

Every UNDRR-on-brand image prompt is three blocks bolted together:

```
[YOUR SUBJECT] + [UNDRR STYLE BLOCK] + [OUTPUT SPEC]
```

Pick a style block based on whether your image has people or not. Pick an output spec based on where the image will sit on the page.

### The UNDRR style block (with people)

Copy this verbatim and paste it after your subject.

> Clearly stylised flat vector editorial illustration, not photo-realistic. Faces in profile, three-quarter view, or obscured — no frontal portraits, no rendered facial detail, no identifiable faces. No children. Mixed team: deliberately varied skin tones appropriate to the named place, ages including older persons, body sizes including larger-bodied adults, hair textures (including textured and curly hair worn naturally), varied clothing within regional norms. **At least one person with a visible disability as a peer** (not a recipient) — "persons with disabilities" is the UN canonical first-mention form; "disabled participant" is acceptable shorthand on subsequent mentions. No "outside expert / local recipient" framing — frame people as peers with named professional roles. Working clothing appropriate to the place; no aid-worker hi-vis vests unless the named role requires them. Palette: Sendai reduced-saturation (#d45363, #f19e6a, #b669ab, #4dc7c6) plus UNDRR Corporate Blue (#004f91). No text, no rendered letters, no logos, no icons; pictogram-only signs without rendered words if signage is needed in frame.

### The UNDRR style block (no people, diagram or background)

> Clearly stylised flat vector illustration. Side-elevation or top-down perspective; avoid corporate-isometric clichés. Recognisable real-world elements appropriate to the named place. White background. Palette: Sendai reduced-saturation (#d45363, #f19e6a, #b669ab, #4dc7c6) plus UNDRR Corporate Blue (#004f91). No text, no rendered letters, no logos, no icons. No decorative gradients, drone overlays, holographic data, or smart-city sci-fi tropes.

### Sub-brand colour swap

For sub-brand work, replace `UNDRR Corporate Blue (#004f91)` in the style block with the sub-brand primary:

| Sub-brand | Primary to substitute |
| --- | --- |
| PreventionWeb | `#0a6969` PreventionWeb teal |
| MCR2030 | `#591a61` MCR2030 purple |
| IRP | `#0f78bf` IRP bright blue |
| ARISE | `#921d2c` ARISE burgundy |
| DELTA Resilience | `#132e48` DELTA dark navy |

<DocCallout variant="warning">
  <strong>Colour swap alone is not enough.</strong> Each sub-brand has visual-emphasis cues that must be layered in — for example PreventionWeb wants real research artifacts on the table; MCR2030 wants plural cities of varied scale; ARISE wants local industry workers as protagonists, never aid-recipient framings. Read the per-property guidance in <LinkTo kind="brand-sub-brand-rationale" story="docs">sub-brand rationale</LinkTo> before writing a sub-brand prompt.
</DocCallout>

### Output spec

Append one of these depending on use case:

- **Hero banner:** `16:6 aspect ratio, focal subject left of centre, negative space right of centre for editorial copy.`
- **News image:** `16:9 aspect ratio, focal subject centred.`
- **Infographic background:** `16:6 aspect ratio, low-contrast, no foreground elements.`
- **Explainer / methodology illustration:** `16:9 aspect ratio, side-elevation perspective, white background.`

### Worked example: from "financing for DRR" to a usable prompt

If your starting brief is "I want something about financing for DRR," answer three questions before you write anything:

1. **Which country?** (Not a region — see Rule 6 in the <LinkTo kind="brand-ai-imagery-policy" story="docs">policy</LinkTo>.) Pick the country your story is actually about, or a representative country if the story is sectoral.
2. **Which level?** Community / sub-national / national-government / regional convening / international. Each implies a different setting and a different cast.
3. **Which roles are in the frame?** Be specific — "finance ministry, disaster management agency, planning ministry" beats "officials." Named roles drive named compositions.

Once you have those three, write your subject as a single specific sentence:

> National-government planning meeting reviewing a multi-year DRR investment plan in Indonesia. Setting: working government office in Jakarta, printed budget projections and risk maps on the table. Mixed planners — finance ministry, disaster management agency, planning ministry — as peers.

Then add the **style block (with people)** verbatim, then add the **output spec** — `16:6 aspect ratio, focal subject left of centre, negative space right of centre for editorial copy.`

Glue them together and you have the prompt that became example D above. The model gets clear instructions, the editorial guardrails are in the prompt, and the output should pass the <LinkTo kind="brand-anti-patterns" story="docs">pre-publish checklist</LinkTo>.

If you cannot answer the three questions, jump to the LLM meta-prompt below — paste your rough brief in and the LLM will ask you the same questions.

## 3. LLM meta-prompt (let an LLM write the image prompt for you)

If you have a rough brief but you don't want to assemble the formula yourself, paste this meta-prompt plus your brief into ChatGPT, Claude, or any general-purpose LLM. The LLM will return a complete UNDRR-on-brand image prompt.

> You are helping a UNDRR communications officer write a prompt for an AI image model (Midjourney, DALL·E, Imagen, Stable Diffusion). Produce one prompt as a single paragraph. Apply every rule below without omission.
>
> **UNDRR standing rules — non-negotiable.**
>
> - Clearly stylised flat vector editorial illustration, not photo-realistic.
> - No identifiable human faces. Faces in profile, three-quarter view, or obscured; no rendered facial detail.
> - **No AI imagery of children, identifiable or not.** If the brief mentions children, decline and suggest the officer commission credited photography with documented guardian consent, or use a child-free composition (caregivers, teachers, infrastructure).
> - No named real people. No specific real events.
> - No rendered text, letters, logos, or icons in the output. If signage is needed in frame, request "pictogram-only signs without rendered words."
> - Specify a named country and a built-environment cue (not a region like "the Pacific" or "sub-Saharan Africa").
> - Deliberately varied skin tones appropriate to the named place; varied ages including older persons (**at least one participant aged 60+ in any multi-person scene, in an active role**); varied body sizes (**including larger-bodied adults in professional roles, not only as background**); varied hair textures (including textured and curly hair worn naturally); varied clothing within regional norms.
> - **Disability inclusion is required, scaled to scene size:** at least one person with a visible disability in groups of three to six; two or more in larger gatherings. Rotate disability type across outputs (mobility, sensory, invisible / chronic). Frame persons with disabilities as peers, not recipients. Do not invent visual neurodivergence stereotypes. (UN canonical first-mention form is "persons with disabilities"; "disabled participants" is acceptable shorthand on subsequent mentions.)
> - Decolonial framing — frame people as peers with named professional roles; never "outside expert / local recipient" and never "white saviour" postures (kneeling beside, demonstrating to, handing to). Counter model defaults explicitly: African = rural, Asian = urban high-tech, Latin American = informal settlement, Middle Eastern = conflict zone, Pacific = beach. If the brief contradicts a default, name the counter-context (urban Nairobi, rural Japan, middle-class São Paulo, university Amman, ministry Suva).
> - **Indigenous representation:** Indigenous Peoples hold collective rights over ceremonial dress, sacred objects, body markings, tattoos, and regalia — do not depict any of those. Indigenous individuals may be depicted in everyday professional and community contexts. If the brief is specifically about Indigenous-led practice, decline and recommend commissioned work.
> - **LGBTIQ+ identity cues:** do not add Pride flags, hand-holding same-sex couples, or explicit symbols to scenes set in countries where this creates criminal or safety risk for people who may be associated with the image. (UN system usage is LGBTIQ+ — "I" for intersex.)
> - **Religious minority visibility:** in named places with significant religious minorities, vary religious dress cues (hijab worn and not worn, kippah, turban, cross, no visible marker).
> - Palette: Sendai reduced-saturation (#d45363, #f19e6a, #b669ab, #4dc7c6) plus the relevant primary — UNDRR Corporate Blue (#004f91) by default, or substitute the sub-brand primary if specified: PreventionWeb teal (#0a6969), MCR2030 purple (#591a61), IRP bright blue (#0f78bf), ARISE burgundy (#921d2c), DELTA dark navy (#132e48).
> - Output spec defaults to 16:6 hero banner unless the brief specifies otherwise.
>
> **Diversity-within-named-place rule.** When the brief names a single country, name the kind of gathering and resolve diversity accordingly: local community context → match the actual demographic composition of the named locality, including resident minorities (ethnic homogeneity is acceptable only where the named locality is genuinely homogeneous, and never as a tool to render resident minorities invisible); national-government context → name the heritage groups explicitly (Bangladesh: Bengali, Adivasi including Chakma, Marma, Garo, Santal; Brazil: Afro-Brazilian, Indigenous, European-Brazilian, Asian-Brazilian; Indonesia: Javanese, Sundanese, Batak, Bugis, Papuan and other heritage groups); regional or international convening hosted in a country → name the participating countries.
>
> **Anti-patterns to avoid in the generated prompt.** Conference rooms; disaster tourism; decorative-only stock; AI-generic isometrics; corporate stock visual clichés (handshake-over-globe, abstract supply-chain swooshes); smart-city sci-fi tropes; "outside expert / local recipient" framing; white-saviour postures; tokenising single-disability representation; invented neurodivergence iconography.
>
> **The officer's brief:** [PASTE YOUR ROUGH BRIEF HERE]
>
> **If the brief is thin** — missing country, level (community / sub-national / national / regional / international), roles in the frame, or output context — ask up to three clarifying questions before producing the prompt. If the brief is complete, return only the finished image prompt as a single paragraph. Do not return commentary or explanations. Do not return a list of rules — apply them inside the prompt.

## 4. Language model prompts (copy and editorial)

These are for drafting headlines, captions, alt text, and tone-shifting copy with a language model. Same principle as above — paste with your text.

### Alt text generator

> Write alt text for the following image in ≤120 characters. UNDRR house style: descriptive, names people if known, names location if known, names what they are doing, no decorative adjectives, plain English. Do not start with "Image of" or "Photo of." Image description: [paste].

### Sentence-case rewriter

> Rewrite the following heading in sentence case per UNDRR house style. Capitalise only the first word, proper nouns, and acronyms. Do not change the meaning. Heading: [paste].

### British-English converter

> Convert the following text from American English to British English (Oxford spelling) per UNDRR Policies and SOPs. Preserve all proper nouns, organisation names, indigenous and place names, transliterations from non-Latin scripts, diacritics (Māori macrons, Spanish accents, Arabic transliteration choices), and quoted speech exactly. Text: [paste].

### Tone-register rewriter

> Rewrite the following copy in **[ONE register]**, where the registers are defined as:
>
> - **Authoritative** — sourced (cite the report, resolution, or dataset by name and date), neutral (state what the evidence shows; let the reader draw the conclusion), precise (full names of frameworks and reports), no institutional fog ("it is important to note that"). For policy summaries, research findings, methodology, official statements.
> - **Warm** — names people, places, jobs, ages; specific sensory detail; quoted speech in people's own words; emotion at the edges. For people stories, community profiles, partner spotlights, recovery narratives.
> - **Hopeful** — forward-looking and evidence-based; collective ("communities," "partners," "member states" named); specific futures ("by 2030," not "in the coming years"); never glosses over what is still hard. For prevention success, recovery progress, anniversary content.
> - **Procedural** — numbered, defined-terms, second-person imperative, prescriptive. For Words into Action guides, methodology, operational updates, technical specifications.
> - **Diplomatic** — multilateral attribution ("championed by [Member State]," "co-led with the Government of…"); acknowledges named states and partners; ceremonial register without being florid. For commemorative observances, member-state-facing statements, Regional Platform openings.
> - **Time-sensitive** — front-loaded (verb in first six words); imperative; concrete (specific actions, times, places); restrained (urgency from precision, not adjectives). For early-warning calls-to-action, EW4All milestones, partner-amplification on behalf of OCHA / IFRC / national authorities, time-bounded launches and deadlines. UNDRR rarely voices live-disaster content itself — do not manufacture an emergency tone UNDRR does not actually use.
>
> Hold the K / A / C voice trio (Knowledgeable, Approachable, Collaborative) regardless of register. Use British English (Oxford spelling). Use sentence case for any headings. Avoid jargon. Em-dashes are fine in body prose; do not use them in UI strings, button labels, or story names. Copy: [paste].

### Headline generator

> Generate three headline options in sentence case for a [news / explainer / event / methodology] page about [topic]. UNDRR house style: front-load the key fact, name a place if possible, name a number if available, no marketing-flavoured language, British English (Oxford spelling). Examples of preferred style: "Cyclone deaths halved across Pacific early warning network." "Eighty hectares of mangrove kept Talcahuano-Sur's port open during the March cyclone."
