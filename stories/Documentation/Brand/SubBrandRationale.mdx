This page goes deeper on each property — the visual emphasis cues, the sub-brand-specific anti-patterns, and the AI imagery prompt variant — for the moments when you are actively producing for that sub-brand.

## PreventionWeb — knowledge platform

**What the live site is doing (May 2026).** Their own masthead: "The risk knowledge hub for the global DRR community." Self-cited service metrics: 45,000 daily entries and weekly alerts; a monthly average of over 400 new content pieces (news, publications, events, training, academic programmes, jobs); 89,000 pieces of content on open datasets, statistics, reports, and collections. The homepage reads as a **live current-awareness feed**, not a static knowledge reference shelf.

**Current programmes.** Early Warnings for All (EW4All) updates collection; the AI + DRR Conference 2026; DRR Academy 2026 (University of Salerno); ongoing UN Plan of Action on DRR for Resilience coverage; rolling calls for proposals and expressions of interest.

**Audience (as they name it).** "DRR community," "DRR professionals," "practitioners," and — explicitly foregrounded — **"Early Career Researchers and Early Career Practitioners."** Time-poor experts who reward scannability first, depth second.

**Register skew.** Knowledgeable + Authoritative — but with a real-time, editorial energy. Hopeful and Warm registers play smaller roles. The site rewards depth-on-demand; the front door rewards quick scanning of what's new.

**Visual emphasis.**

- **Primary accent**: PreventionWeb teal (`#0a6969`), replacing UNDRR Corporate Blue.
- **Photography**: methodology in progress (researchers reviewing maps and printed reports, fieldwork as the work itself, monitoring instruments). Real working artifacts on tables and in hands.
- **Illustration**: methodology diagrams, framework explanations, system maps. Less stylised flourish than core UNDRR; more diagrammatic restraint.
- **Data viz**: prominent, sourced, methodologically transparent. Reduced-saturation Sendai colours for sequential scales; PreventionWeb teal for the primary series.

**What flexes that wouldn't on core UNDRR.** Longer page lengths are acceptable (researchers will read). Citations can be denser. Dataset and report pages can use chart-led heroes rather than people-led heroes. **First-class content types include calls (for proposals, expressions of interest) and jobs** — both rare elsewhere in the UNDRR family, both expected here.

**Sub-brand-specific anti-patterns.**

- **Avoid**: glowing-network "knowledge platform" stock illustration; abstract "data" backgrounds without specific data; lightbulb iconography; static "reference shelf" framing that ignores the live current-awareness feed character.
- **Prefer**: real research artifacts (printed reports, datasets on screens, methodology diagrams, citation-rich tables); recent-content rails that respect the front-door scanning behaviour.

**AI imagery prompt variant.** Swap the primary colour in the standard prompt formula to PreventionWeb teal (`#0a6969`). Bolt on the visual-emphasis cues above (real research artifacts, methodology in progress). See the <LinkTo kind="brand-ai-imagery-prompts" story="docs">prompt cookbook</LinkTo> for the formula.

## MCR2030 — cities and local governments

**What the live site is doing (May 2026).** Self-description: "Making Cities Resilient 2030 (MCR2030) is a unique cross-stakeholder initiative for improving local resilience through advocacy, sharing knowledge and experiences, establishing mutually reinforcing city-to-city learning networks, injecting technical expertise, connecting multiple layers of government and building partnerships." The dominant content type is **structured assessment tooling**, not peer storytelling.

**Current programmes and tools.** The three-stage **Resilience Roadmap** (Stage A awareness → Stage B planning → Stage C mainstreaming) is the central organising metaphor. The **Disaster Resilience Scorecard for Cities** (117 indicators 0–5) is the flagship knowledge product, with specialised addenda: **Disaster Displacement**, **Climate Resilience**, **Multi-Hazard Early Warning Systems**. ISO 37123 indicator alignment. The **MCR2030 Dashboard** is a closed working platform for signed-up cities; **OpenMCR** is the public live data view of cities along the roadmap. Dashboard Orientation webinar series ongoing through 2026.

**Audience (as they name it).** "Cities," "local governments," "participating entities," and **"partners"** — partner organisations are co-protagonists with cities. The live site speaks to **cities-as-institutions** and to partner organisations more than it speaks to individual mayors and municipal planners (which is the *aspirational* audience our brand work tends to assume).

**Register skew.** Collaborative + Warm, with an instrument-led foundation. Authoritative remains the floor for any methodology or framework reference. The "first-person plural collective voice" permission is a recommendation; it is not visibly exercised on the live site today.

**Visual emphasis.**

- **Primary accent**: MCR2030 purple (`#591a61`), replacing UNDRR Corporate Blue.
- **Photography**: cities at varied scales (a small island town next to a mid-sized inland city next to a coastal megacity); municipal staff and partner-organisation representatives as protagonists, not central-government figures; the work itself rather than the city skyline.
- **Illustration**: peer-comparison framings (named cities side by side); urban-systems explainers; accessible infrastructure (ramps, tactile paving, accessible evacuation routes) shown as part of the resilient city.
- **Data viz**: city-comparison charts; toolkit visualisations; replicability indicators. Scorecard score distributions across cities, when the source data supports it.

**What flexes that wouldn't on core UNDRR.** First-person plural collective voice ("we as cities," "what local governments are doing") is allowed. Specific named cities can carry the page. **Scorecard scores and Resilience Roadmap stages can be cited inline** — the audience is fluent in both.

**Sub-brand-specific anti-patterns.**

- **Avoid**: starchitect skylines; single-megacity dominance; "smart city" sci-fi imagery; glass-walled government boardrooms; framing the MCR2030 brand as if it speaks only to cities (the partner-organisation co-protagonist is missing).
- **Prefer**: working municipal offices appropriate to the named city; staff in the field; toolkits and templates as visual subjects; partner organisations named alongside cities in case studies.

**AI imagery prompt variant.** Swap the primary colour in the standard prompt formula to MCR2030 purple (`#591a61`). Bolt on the visual-emphasis cues above (plural cities of varied scale, municipal staff as protagonists). See the <LinkTo kind="brand-ai-imagery-prompts" story="docs">prompt cookbook</LinkTo> for the formula.

## IRP — recovery

**What the live site is doing (May 2026).** Self-description: "The International Recovery Platform (IRP) is a global partnership working to strengthen knowledge, and share experiences and lessons on building back better in recovery, rehabilitation, and reconstruction. It is a joint initiative of United Nations organizations, international financial institutions, national and local governments, and non-governmental organizations engaged in disaster recovery." Established 2005 in Kobe; supports Sendai Priority 4.

**The brand verb is "Build Back Better."** This is not framing; it is the IRP top-level navigation concept ("All Recovery Resources," "Guidance for Disaster Recovery") and the spine of every IRP publication and event. Any IRP artifact that does not earn its place against this verb is mis-scoped.

**Current programmes and flagship content.** The **Recovery Help Desk** (request-driven national and subnational support — a distinctive *service*, not just content); the annual **International Recovery Forum**; the **IRP Herald** publication series; the **Guidance Notes on Recovery** series (launched 2010, still active); **Priority Actions to Enhance Readiness for Resilient Recovery** (launched at WRRC, 3 June 2025); HNPW 2026 theme "Beyond relief — Building resilience through response."

**Audience (as they name it).** "Recovery planners, decision-makers, and implementers"; "national and subnational governments"; "UN organizations, international financial institutions… NGOs engaged in disaster recovery." Multilateral-partnership-flavoured — not only government recovery offices. The audience composition mirrors IRP's own structure as a partnership.

**Register skew (recommended pending IRP team confirmation).** Authoritative + forward-looking, anchored to the "build back better" verb. IRP has historically leaned methodology-and-framework-heavy (closer to PreventionWeb). The earlier proposal of a "Hopeful" skew is not visible in IRP's own materials and is withdrawn here; the live tone is partnership-secretariat and service-flavoured (Help Desk), not tonal hope. Confirm with the IRP team and UNDRR Communications before treating any register skew as standing guidance.

**Visual emphasis.**

- **Primary accent**: IRP bright blue (`#0f78bf`), replacing UNDRR Corporate Blue.
- **Photography**: recovery in progress, not the disaster — community kitchens in re-opened schools, construction visible, monitoring activity; named partner organisations at the table.
- **Illustration**: the four-phase recovery framework (response, early recovery, recovery, long-term resilience) as a horizontal flow; methodology diagrams; the build-back-better verb foregrounded in callouts.
- **Data viz**: recovery progress over time; sequential metrics; before-and-during-and-after comparisons (never just before-and-after).

**What flexes that wouldn't on core UNDRR.** A longer time horizon is allowed in storytelling — IRP content can span months and years explicitly. Recovery-specific terminology (early recovery, build-back-better) can sit foregrounded with definitions linked. **The Help Desk as a service offer can be surfaced as a direct CTA** — rare elsewhere in the UNDRR family.

**Sub-brand-specific anti-patterns.**

- **Avoid**: ribbon-cutting imagery; before-and-after shock comparisons that exclude the during; distressed survivors; rubble as the dominant frame; framing IRP as a content publisher only (it is also a service).
- **Prefer**: agency through recovery action; methodology and monitoring as visible subjects; named partners as protagonists; the build-back-better verb in headlines.

**AI imagery prompt variant.** Swap the primary colour in the standard prompt formula to IRP bright blue (`#0f78bf`). Bolt on the visual-emphasis cues above (recovery in progress, named partner organisations, build-back-better framing). See the <LinkTo kind="brand-ai-imagery-prompts" story="docs">prompt cookbook</LinkTo> for the formula.

## ARISE — private sector alliance

**What the live site is doing (May 2026).** Self-description: "ARISE, the Private Sector Alliance for Disaster Resilient Societies, is a network of private sector entities established by the UN Office for Disaster Risk Reduction (UNDRR)." Live tagline: **"Resilient Business, Sustainable World."** Lead hero: "The private sector can help prevent disasters / ARISE members big and small work together to prevent disasters so their enterprises and communities can thrive."

<HighlightBox variant="warning">
  <strong>The live tagline "Resilient Business, Sustainable World" is corporate-association-flavoured.</strong> It would not pass the "would this read on undrr.org" test. Surface this in conversations with the ARISE secretariat — the tagline is itself an anti-pattern artefact worth revisiting.
</HighlightBox>

**Current programmes and flagship content.** The flagship 2025/26 project is **Strengthening the Disaster Resilience of SMEs** (Barcelona / Bridgetown / Sendai). UNDRR & ARISE Japan joint symposium 2026 (15th anniversary of Great East Japan Earthquake). Barcelona SME Resilience Forum. SME-resilience webinar series with regional lenses (Caribbean, urban). Member stories (e.g. New World Capital Inc., Steamatic). Barbados tourism-sector resilience.

**Audience (as they name it).** "Private sector entities," "members big and small," and — most foregrounded in 2025/26 — **SMEs** ("the backbone of economies, 90% of business, 60–70% of employment, 50% of GDP"). Plus "regional and national networks," "business and other public and private partners," "Governments and other stakeholders." The board-level audience of "insurers, finance institutions" that earlier ARISE materials cite is *aspirational*; the live audience is SMEs and member companies in regional networks.

**Network structure.** ARISE is a network with **regional and national chapters** — ARISE Japan, ARISE Barcelona, ARISE Barbados, ARISE-US, and growing. This membership-association structure is a defining identity feature and shapes the voice (join / member story / submit / newsletter CTAs).

**Register skew (recommended pending ARISE secretariat confirmation).** Authoritative + Hopeful with a continuity frame, **plus a peer-network register** for member-to-member content (member stories, newsletters, regional-chapter updates). Of the five sub-brands, ARISE is the most at risk of voice drift — toward business-development, marketing-flavoured, or transactional framings. Authoritative should be non-negotiable for that reason. This characterisation is a strategist's read of failure modes (the "Resilient Business, Sustainable World" tagline is the canonical example); surface to ARISE secretariat and UNDRR Communications before treating as standing guidance.

**Visual emphasis.**

- **Primary accent**: ARISE burgundy red (`#921d2c`), replacing UNDRR Corporate Blue.
- **Photography**: industry workers as protagonists — SME owners and staff in their actual workplaces (shopfront, workshop, supply-chain hub, hotel, restaurant), not corporate boardrooms. Multi-party meetings as peers (member-to-member, not aid-recipient).
- **Illustration**: business-case visualisations grounded in real SME continuity scenarios; ROI explainers with sector specificity; named-industry partnership maps.
- **Data viz**: continuity ROI; risk reduction in financial terms; sector-specific impact charts; SME-sector concentration figures.

**What flexes that wouldn't on core UNDRR (recommended permissions, pending ARISE secretariat sign-off).** Financial framings are explicitly allowed (USD figures for avoided continuity disruption, sector-specific risk metrics). Industry-specific terminology can be foregrounded with definitions linked. Member-story content type is allowed (rare elsewhere in the UNDRR family — Mangrove components support it, brand position should as well).

**Sub-brand-specific anti-patterns.**

- **Avoid**: an outside expert in a hi-vis vest directing local labour; corporate stock visual clichés (handshake-over-globe, abstract supply-chain swooshes); marketing-flavoured taglines (the live "Resilient Business, Sustainable World" is the cautionary example); language that drifts toward business-development voice. Every ARISE artifact must still pass the "would this read on undrr.org" test.
- **Prefer**: local SME owners and workers as protagonists; named regional chapters as co-protagonists; partnership-model framings; specific evidence with named partners; member-story format where the story earns it.

**AI imagery prompt variant.** Swap the primary colour in the standard prompt formula to ARISE burgundy (`#921d2c`). Bolt on the visual-emphasis cues above (local SME owners and workers as protagonists, partnership models, peer framings — never aid-recipient framings). See the <LinkTo kind="brand-ai-imagery-prompts" story="docs">prompt cookbook</LinkTo> for the formula.

## DELTA Resilience — data and methodology

**What the live site is doing (May 2026).** Self-description (live HTML): "**DELTA Resilience — Disaster & Hazardous Events, Losses and Damages Tracking & Analysis.** A toolkit to collect, manage and analyze hazardous events and losses and damages data to support disaster risk reduction." Three-verb spine: **Collect → Analyze → Insights for action.**

**This is a software product, not a methodology journal.** Primary CTAs on the homepage are "Sign-in" / "Log in" and "View documentation." Six advertised key features: Analytics, Monitoring, Data management & integration, Losses and damages overview, Connected disaster data, Baseline & contextual data integration. Core navigation: Partners, Methodologies, Technical specifications, Support. The genre is SaaS-style product page.

**Lineage.** DELTA is the **successor to DesInventar** — 110+ country databases since the 1990s. That lineage carries institutional credibility on day one.

**Joint initiative.** DELTA is **co-developed by UNDRR + UNDP + WMO** — not a UNDRR-only product. The structural identity claim matters for partner-acknowledgement copy and for understanding why methodology decisions are tri-partner discussions.

**Current rollouts.** Regional adoption in the **Caribbean** (Bridgetown workshop, April 2026, 13 countries) and the **Arab States** (Doha workshop, October 2025, 18 Member States). Training-of-trainers events through 2026. Presented to the IAEG-SDGs at UN Statistics.

**Audience (as they name it).** Implicit on the live site — the user is someone who collects, manages, and analyzes losses and damages data. From UNDRR's own DELTA pages: **"national statistical offices, disaster management authorities, planning ministries, data producers and users."** This is a **national-government data-system-operator audience**, not the methodology-researcher audience the earlier framing assumed.

**Register skew.** Authoritative as the dominant register, **product-page genre**. Knowledgeable is constant; Hopeful and Warm appear sparingly. DELTA reads as a serious operational tool — not as a journal, not as a reference shelf.

**Visual emphasis.**

- **Primary accent**: DELTA dark navy (`#132e48`), replacing UNDRR Corporate Blue.
- **Photography**: real methodological artifacts (assessment frameworks on paper, monitoring matrices, validation steps); national-government data analysts and disaster management authority staff at work — not generic "researchers with laptops."
- **Illustration**: methodology diagrams; analytical workflows; framework decompositions; the Collect → Analyze → Insights verb spine as a visible motif.
- **Data viz**: highest-density viz of the five sub-brands. Source citations required, methodology footnotes expected.
- **UI patterns**: respects authenticated-experience surfaces (login, documentation, support) — the brand lives partly behind authentication, which is unusual in the UNDRR family.

**What flexes that wouldn't on core UNDRR.** Longer methodology pages and explicit appendix sections are acceptable. Technical terminology can sit foregrounded. **Product-page conventions** (key-features grid, "Sign-in" CTA, "View documentation" alongside, technical specifications navigation) are allowed and expected. **Tri-partner acknowledgement** (UNDRR + UNDP + WMO co-branding contexts) must be observed in policy and methodology pages.

**Sub-brand-specific anti-patterns.**

- **Avoid**: glowing data-visualisation tropes; AI-generic "researcher with laptop" stock; decorative analytics imagery without underlying methodology; framing DELTA as a UNDRR-only product (it is a tri-partner system); positioning DELTA as a publication or journal (it is a software system with logins).
- **Prefer**: real analytical artifacts; methodology in progress; national-government data system operators as the people in frame; the DesInventar lineage cited where it carries credibility; the Collect → Analyze → Insights spine visible.

**AI imagery prompt variant.** Swap the primary colour in the standard prompt formula to DELTA dark navy (`#132e48`). Bolt on the visual-emphasis cues above (real methodological artifacts, national-government data system operators in the work).
